Why these colors?

The 4 colors represent 4 perspectives or ways of knowing. Carl Jung, the Swiss psychiatrist, referred to these as functions of one’s personality. Each contributes to overall understanding:

  • Sensing (green) grasps what is…what is currently happening or has happened, as perceived by the senses, typically in specific, concrete or literal terms;
  • Intuition (yellow) infers what possibilities may ensue from what is, wondering where it came from, where it’s going, and what it potentially means;
  • Feeling (red) determines its value, desirability or appropriateness, evaluating to what extent ‘it’ is in harmony with group norms or personal values;
  • Thinking (blue) judges its clarity, correctness or reasonableness against objective criteria, and identifies logical consequences and next steps.

From his work with patients, Jung realized that each color was correlated with a particular function of one’s personality; hence, the use of these colors.
