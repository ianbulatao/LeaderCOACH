Why is equal ideal

Carl Jung theorized that 1 of these 4 functions predominates in your personality, and another plays a supporting role; the other 2 functions remain largely untapped. (Think of your personality as a pie chart: 47% is your dominant role (color); 30% is your supporting actor; 17% is your third-rate function, and 7% is your least accessed and developed part.) Consequently, we tend to see things from our unique, yet limited point of view, and tend to explore only 2, maybe 3, of the 4 perspectives; hence, the need for a holistic view of a situation or issue.

Carl Jung said, For complete orientation (understanding) all four functions should contribute equally:

  • Thinking should facilitate cognition and judgment,
  • Feeling should tell us how and to what extent a thing is important or unimportant to us,
  • Sensing should convey concrete reality to us through seeing, hearing, tasting, etc., and
  • Intuition should enable us to divine the hidden possibilities in the background, since these too belong to the complete picture of a given situation.
